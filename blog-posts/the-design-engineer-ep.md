# The Team
And so it happened on this beautiful day, there I was on my bed, and voila a message came in stating that the necessary, that I needed to travel down, we have a drone project. okayy, I packed up the next day I was at the train station, I missed the train, so I took a bus, I arrived to the location in the rain, My friend picked up and took me to the office, I think I was the second to the last person to arrive kinda like that though, we did the whole meet and greet thing, Two Materials Engineer that was me and my Friend, A mechanical engineer, a drone pilot and his apprentice, a physicist, a security engineer, and the CEO and his PA. At first, I thought what role do am I supposed to play here? I am not into that mechatronic stuff, and I just kept on having thoughts overthinking the whole thing as usual, and so we had a meeting where we discussed and I think I was silent for most part of the meeting, just listening. By the end of the meeting, we set the deadline and everything, and from the meeting I knew exactly the role to play the Design Engineer.
## Design Engineer
Well, I wanted to tell you who a design engineer is but nah, google it no time, so it begins I was going to design the prototype therefore I needed to take precise measurement, and look out for accuracy where necessary, sketching in 2d and all and mind you this was a prototype design because we were trying a protype first of a UAV VTOL Drone, the drone pilot was also the drone expert alongside the physicist, so they were working on the connections and all I was working on the Design, My Friend was in charge of Quality Control one of the many functions of a Materials Engineer, The Mechanical Engineer was all about the Engine as well, any way...I love the dynamic and division of labour, we all knew what we needed to do, so there I had my measurements ready now it's time to design.
### Design Dilemma 
You know one of the dilemmas of designing is knowing what to design and picturing it in your head as it should look like, but when you get to the sheet or front of the screen you are clueless on how to start yeah well same thing here, I find it hard to design it was like my chakra was blocked, I need serenity and some kinda calm, but none of that was available, and I couldn't really see through, so instead I started with paper sketch as usual, infact that is the fastest way I know how to unlock my chakra to allow me think and reason fast for design, and so it started to make sense on paper and I was feeling it, but on SolidWorks it was something else hahaha, anyway I just calm down and close the laptop but my brain was already running the whole thing, like seeing a bunch of numbers in the air, and before you know it I knew what I had to do.
### The UAV VTOL
At this point in time my brain pathways and neural network were all clear and heighten firing at high rate, in the silent moment of the night I was on my pc designing better than Leonardo Davinci yes, Because Davinci doesn't know a thing about using SolidWorks, but I do, and by morning I was already done my roommate woke up and they could see an art staring back at them from the bed. we went to the office, and everyone was intrigued, amazed and excited about the design, The Mechanical Engineer who is also a designer appreciated it as well and complimented my work at some point we did design some part together.
### NACA Standard
I didn't really know NACA standard for aircraft wings was a thing, till I got to the point where I needed to design the wing and that was when I started to learn about the flat, semi flat, and all that, I had experience with Aircraft before now, during my time at the Nigerian Air Force, but the wings design principle didn't come up partly because I was into more of repairs of the Aircraft Airframe, and all That stuff like NDT, Corrosion Engineering etc... but here now I learned that and they have numbers and all that, I had to go to the NACA site thanks to the Mechanical Engineer and I got the necessary details before you know it I understood the whole thing, and ready to design again.
## FEA Simulation
Furthermore, after the whole thing with the NACA Standard thing, and designing the wings I needed to perform a Finite Element Analysis, to see how the whole thing fair out with Aerodynamics, another tricky part, and this was important because it determines a lot especially given the shape of the drone, in case we needed more streamlining or whatsoever, well I tested everything I had some errors, fix them were necessary, I even tested again including the material which was Carbon Fibre, and it went smoothly, result was great at the end.
## Materials Engineer
Back to the Materials Selection situation we were moulding with POP and we used Carbon Fiber, as the external body material, we used hand layup method to lay the fiber, and we made use of Epoxy Resin and Hardener, with a ratio 2:1, at this point we lay the stuff little did I know that those fibers could be itchy as well always use gloves, and so we began I did layup, alongside the drone technicians and at some point the fiber wasn't enough and the stuff was expensive we had to order for more, but at the end of the day it was all wrapped up, although it tends to add more weight compared to when it was just the POP not surprised.
## Conclusion
You see basically at the end of the day I didn't do anything that was outside my field or something that didn't require the knowledge of a materials engineer, anyway we all completed our various part and before you know it the drone was up in the sky and that was a proud moment for me.
